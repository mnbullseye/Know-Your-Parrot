Cockatiel

General Cockatiel Information

Temperament

  • Social, affectionate, intelligent.
  • Known for their distinct crest, which signals their mood.
  • Crest Flat/Slightly Raised: Relaxed, happy.
  • Crest Fully Erect: Alert, excited, or cautious.
  • Crest Pinned Down: Angry, frightened, or aggressive.
  • Vocalization: Whistling, chattering, and simple phrases/songs.

Minimum Size

A spacious cage is essential for your bird to flap its wings and move around.

Single Cockatiel

Min: 24" L x 18" W x 24" H

Two Cockatiels

Increase width & depth significantly (e.g., 30" L x 20" W x 36" H)

Essential Cage Furnishings

  • Perches: Variety of diameters (natural branches best). Avoid sandpaper.
  • Bowls: Stainless steel or ceramic. Place away from droppings.
  • Toys: Rotate chewing, foraging, and stimulation toys regularly.

Environment & Safety

Dangers to Be Cautious Of

Hazard
Risk
Prevention
Non-Stick Cookware (Teflon)
Releases toxic fumes when overheated (at $\approx 500^{\circ}$F / $260^{\circ}$C), causing immediate respiratory failure.
NEVER use non-stick cookware in the same house. Use stainless steel or cast iron.
Open Doors/Windows
Risk of escape.
Keep doors and windows closed or securely screened when the bird is out of its cage.
Toxic Household Items
Ingestion or contact can be lethal.
Keep cleaning chemicals, pesticides, glues, and human medications locked away.
Other Pets (Cats/Dogs)
Can injure or kill the cockatiel, even if playing.
Always supervise interactions, and keep them separated when unsupervised.

Creating a Safe, Stimulating Environment

  • Location: Place cage in a high-traffic area (living room) away from drafts/direct sunlight.
  • Sleep: Requires $10$‚Äì$12$ hours of uninterrupted sleep. Cover the cage completely in a dark, quiet area.
  • Bathing: Provide a shallow dish of water or a misting spray a few times a week.
  • Out-of-Cage Time: Allow at least 1-2 hours of supervised free-flight/play time daily for exercise and socialization.
